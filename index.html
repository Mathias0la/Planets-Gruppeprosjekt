Some facts about Neptune

Overview

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun in our Solar System. Known as an "ice giant," it's named after the Roman god of the sea, fitting for a planet thatâ€™s a stunning, deep blue.


Discovered in 1846, Neptune was the first planet found through mathematical prediction rather than direct observation due to its gravitational influence on nearby Uranus.

Key Facts

Distance from the Sun: About 4.5 billion kilometers (2.8 billion miles)

Orbital Period (Year Length): 165 Earth years

Diameter: Approximately 49,244 kilometers (30,598 miles)

Day Length: Roughly 16 hours

Moons: 14 known moons, with Triton being the largest and most geologically active
